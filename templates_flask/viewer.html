Machine Learning Pipeline Overview

This system integrates 12 ML models into a unified scheduling pipeline. Patient data flows through feature engineering (22 raw fields → 92 features), then through prediction models that estimate no-show risk, treatment duration, and intervention effectiveness. These predictions feed into a multi-objective OR-Tools CP-SAT optimizer that balances 6 competing objectives to produce fair, efficient schedules.

All models are trained on SACT v4.0 compliant synthetic data (1,899 historical records) and recalibrate automatically from NHS open data sources. The system is designed as an intelligent layer on top of existing NHS systems (ChemoCare/ChemoSchedule).

How the ML Pipeline Works

Step 1: Feature Engineering
22 raw patient fields are transformed into 92 ML features including temporal patterns, geographic risk, behavioral history, and interaction terms (e.g., distance × weather).
Step 2: Ensemble Prediction
Three base models (Random Forest + Gradient Boosting + XGBoost) are combined via stacking. A GRU sequence model captures patient attendance trajectories for those with 5+ appointments.
Step 3: Uncertainty Quantification
Conformal Prediction provides guaranteed coverage intervals (P(Y∈C) ≥ 90%). MC Dropout decomposes uncertainty into epistemic (model) vs aleatoric (data noise).
Step 4: Multi-Objective Optimization
OR-Tools CP-SAT solver maximizes a weighted objective across 6 goals: priority, utilization, no-show risk, waiting time, robustness, and travel distance. Fairness constraints ensure scheduling doesn't disadvantage patients by age, gender, or location.

Multi-Objective Optimization Weights

The scheduler balances 6 competing objectives. Weights can be adjusted to generate different trade-off solutions (Pareto frontier). The NHS 62-day target drives the waiting time weight.

NHS Open Data & Auto-Learning Pipeline

Auto-update is active. Background scheduler checks NHS sources every 24 hours (first check 5 min after startup). When new data is found, drift detection runs automatically. If distribution shift is detected, models recalibrate at the appropriate level. 8 datasets available now; SACT v4.0 collection commenced 1 April 2026 (rollout phase — first complete dataset August 2026).

Recalibration Levels:
Level 0: Real-time parameter refresh (weather/traffic) — no downtime
Level 1: Monthly baseline recalibration (CWT data) — no downtime
Level 2: Quarterly feature weight update — <1 min
Level 3: Full model retrain (on drift) — 2-5 min

Fairness Constraints (Equality Act 2010)

The optimizer enforces scheduling fairness across protected characteristics. Demographic Parity ensures equal scheduling rates across age, gender, and distance groups. The Disparate Impact Ratio (Four-Fifths Rule) guarantees no group is scheduled at less than 80% the rate of any other. Equal Opportunity ensures that among patients who would attend, scheduling rates are equal regardless of group.

Inverse RL — Learned Pareto Preferences

Instead of fixed weights, the system learns clinician preferences from past manual overrides of optimiser-proposed schedules. Under a pairwise-softmax choice model θ* = argmax Σ log σ(θ·(Zmanual − Zproposed)) with θ ≥ 0, the weight vector that best explains observed overrides is recovered and written back into the CP-SAT objective.
